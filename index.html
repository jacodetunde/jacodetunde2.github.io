Credit Risk Analysis

Loans are an integral part of modern society. It presents opportunities and poses a serious challenge for banks. It creates revenues with interest rate generated, and other hand, people default payment, thus, makes a loss of revenues. However, machine learning can analyze large datasets to predict risk and inform the decision to approve or reject a loan application. Usually, banks traditionally rely on income, credit scores, and collateral assets to assess lending risk. The challenge is that much data can be left out, which could have aided the lending decisions. In this project, I used the credit card dataset from LendingClub, a peer-to-peer lending services company, to analyze. I used Python and Scikit-learn libraries to build algorithms and predict credit risk. I finally evaluated the following machine learning models:

  • Naive Random Oversampling
  • SMOTE Oversampling
  • Undersampling
  • SMOTEENN
  • Balanced Random Forest Classifier
  • Easy Ensemble AdaBoost Classifier.
    • I then compared evaluated the model to assess how each model works in classifying and predicting the risks.

    Insurance Marketing Engagement

    In this project, I used descriptive analysis to draw insight into the customer behavior of an insurance company to their marketing calls. I used logistic regression to understand what attributes of customers drive higher engagement rates. I started by analyzing the data to better understand and identify noticeable patterns in the data using matplotlib and pandas to visualize the data and then run logistic regression. The results of the logistic regression show that, Income, Monthly Premium Auto, Months Since Last Claim, Months Since Policy Inception , Number of Open Complaints, Number of Policies, and Gender variables are significant at 0.05 and have a negative relationship with engagement. That means the higher the customer's income, the less likely the customer will be engaged with marketing calls.

    Unsupervised Machine Learning Clustering of Cryptocurrencies
